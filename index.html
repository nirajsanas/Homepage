About Me

I am currently pursuing a Bachelor of Science degree in Physics at Fergusson College (Autonomous). I am engaged in the study of theoretical and computational physics.

I am an active member of the Astro Club, a vibrant society where we delve into astrophysics and studies related to physics.

My research interests lies in field of Relativistic Astrophysics with a particular focus on Black Holes and Gravitational Waves.

Curriculum vitae

Experience

ISRO's Astrosat and Black Hole

Presented an exhibit on ASTROSAT, India's first multiwavelength observatory and detection of Black Holes by it at IUCAA. Also explained Black Hole behavior to over 10,000 people

Spectroscopy and Planetary Missions

Presented and won the 3rd prize for work on the atmospheric analysis of Mars and Venus using Astronomical Spectroscopy. Designed a 3D model of the NASA's Spirit-Opportunity Rover

Exoplanets and Detection Methods

Awarded 2nd prize for presenting on exoplanets and the method of detection, particular on WASP-96b. Our team designed the NASA's JUNO spacecraft sent to Jupiter

Gravitational Wave Data Analysis

Completed LIGO's Gravitational Wave Open Data Workshop, using data from LIGO and VIRGO. Learned to generate plots and apply matched filtering techniques to identify transient signals

NASA Exoplanet Watch

Analyzed two planets, CoRoT-1 b and WASP-36 b, as part of NASA's Exoplanet Hunt Citizen Science project. Using EXOTIC, a Python-based software, plotted various graphs for analysis

Computational Astrophysics

Participated in the computational programming course offered by Spartificial and FreeCodeCamp in collaboration, where I plotted various graphs on the Hertzsprung-Russell diagram
